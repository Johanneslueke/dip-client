# 50-Year Fraktion Collaboration via Drucksachen (WP7-21)

**Analysis Date:** December 17, 2025  
**Timeframe:** 1972-2025 (50 years, 15 wahlperioden)  
**Dataset:** Complete drucksache collaboration WP7-21

---

## Executive Summary

This analysis examines fraktion collaboration through **joint drucksache authorship** across 50 years of Bundestag history (WP7-21, 1972-2025). Unlike vorgang initiatives (which track parliamentary procedures and questions), drucksachen represent formal legislative documents—bills, motions, resolutions, and proposals—making them a more precise indicator of voluntary policy collaboration.

**Key Findings:**

- **4,231 joint fraktion drucksachen** over 50 years across 15 wahlperioden
- **CDU/CSU + FDP most collaborative pair** (2,189 joint drucksachen, 26.2%)
- **Peak collaboration: 1998-2009** red-green era (1,461 joint drucksachen)
- **WP18 (2013-2017) Grand Coalition:** 154 joint drucksachen, CDU/CSU+SPD dominance
- **70% substantive policy documents** (43.3% Anträge, 26.9% Gesetzentwürfe)
- **37.3% bilateral collaborations** (2 fraktionen only) vs 6.7% in vorgang analysis
- **AfD complete isolation:** Only 1 bilateral drucksache in 8 years (WP19-21)

## Part 9: Comparison with Vorgang Initiative Analysis

| Metric                         | Vorgang Initiatives (WP7-21)    | Drucksachen (WP7-21)                  | Insight                                      |
| ------------------------------ | ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| **Total Joint Collaborations** | 44,284                          | 4,231                                 | **10x volume difference**                    |
| **Top Pair**                   | CDU/CSU + SPD (7,502, 20.2%)    | CDU/CSU + FDP (2,189, 26.2%)          | Different partnerships dominate              |
| **Document Types**             | 60% Kleine Anfragen (questions) | 70% Anträge + Gesetzentwürfe (policy) | **Procedural vs policy focus**               |
| **Bilateral Rate**             | 6.7%                            | 37.3%                                 | **5.5x more selective**                      |
| **AfD Collaboration**          | 611 with CDU/CSU, 587 with SPD  | 1 bilateral total                     | **Procedural inclusion vs policy exclusion** |
| **Historical Growth**          | 46x increase (WP7→WP20)         | Peaked in 1998-2009, declining since  | Different trajectories                       |

**Key Discovery: Dual Nature of Fraktion Collaboration**

1. **Vorgang Initiatives (Procedural):**

   - High volume (44,284)
   - Dominated by oversight questions (60%)
   - Low bilateral rate (6.7%)—mostly multi-party participation
   - AfD participates normally (600+ with each major party)
   - **Pattern:** Mandatory procedural inclusion

2. **Drucksachen (Policy):**
   - Lower volume (4,231)
   - Dominated by bills and motions (70%)
   - High bilateral rate (37.3%)—selective partnerships
   - AfD nearly excluded (1 bilateral in 8 years)
   - **Pattern:** Voluntary policy collaboration

**Interpretation:** AfD is **procedurally included** in parliamentary work (committee questions, oversight) but **politically isolated** from voluntary policy partnerships. Other fraktionen collaborate with AfD when required by parliamentary procedure but refuse bilateral policy collaborations that would legitimize AfD as a normal political partner.

## Conclusion

This 50-year analysis of fraktion collaboration via drucksachen reveals:

1. **CDU/CSU + FDP Historic Partnership:** 1,308 bilateral drucksachen over 50 years represents unparalleled long-term policy alignment, forged during Kohl era and maintained through changing governments.

2. **Peak Collaboration Era:** 1998-2009 red-green government (Grüne + SPD: 867 bilateral) produced most collaborative period, with opposition also cooperating significantly.

3. **WP18 Grand Coalition Effect:** While overall collaboration was low (154, 5.32%), the CDU/CSU+SPD partnership dominated (144 joint drucksachen), effectively marginalizing opposition through government consensus.

4. **Collaboration Decline:** Sharp drop from 1,461 (1998-2009) to 299 (2017-2025) reflects increasing polarization, especially after AfD's entry in WP19.

5. **Substantive Policy Focus:** 70% of joint drucksachen are bills and motions (vs 60% questions in vorgang analysis), revealing drucksachen as true policy partnerships.

6. **High Bilateral Rate:** 37.3% (vs vorgang 6.7%) shows drucksache collaboration requires deliberate two-party partnership, not procedural participation.

7. **AfD Complete Isolation:** Only 1 bilateral drucksache in 8 years demonstrates political quarantine—AfD is procedurally included but policy-excluded.

**Final Insight:** The dual nature of fraktion collaboration—**procedural inclusion** (vorgang) vs **policy exclusion** (drucksache)—reveals how parliamentary democracies manage extremist parties: including them in mandatory oversight functions while quarantining them from voluntary legislative partnerships that would signal political legitimation.
